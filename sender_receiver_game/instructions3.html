How does the matching of Player As and Player Bs work?

At the beginning of the experiment, you will be randomly allocated into groups of ten participants—five Player As and five Player Bs. In every round of the experiment, Player As and Player Bs are randomly re-matched into five pairs. This means that in any round, any Player A in your matching group can be matched with any Player B in your matching group.

The matching for each round is done by the computer. The computer will randomly select a Player A and a Player B to create each group. For an illustration see the video below.
