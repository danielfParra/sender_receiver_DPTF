How does the matching of Player As and Player Bs work?

At the beginning of the experiment, you will be randomly allocated into groups of ten participants-five Player As and five Player Bs. In every round of the experiment, Player As and Player Bs are randomly re-matched into five pairs. This means that in any round, you can be matched with any Player B in your group.

[Think it would be good to have a picture here titled “Your matching group” and then have stylized avatars of 5 Player As and 5 Player Bs.]

Summary: Each round of the game goes as follows:

  1. The computer generates a secret number.
  2. The Player A sends a message to the Player B.
  3. The Player B receives the message and guesses the secret number.
  4. Both of you receive feedback.

The Player A’s expected bonus is higher when the Player B guesses a higher number. The Player B’s expected bonus is higher if the guess is closer to the secret number. The Player A doesn't know the secret number when they send the message. The Player B knows the Player A doesn't know the secret number.

The experiment ends after {{Constants.num_rounds}} of the game.
