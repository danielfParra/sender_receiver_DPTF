How will the payoffs be determined?

Of the {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds of the experiment, the computer will randomly choose half of them, which will be considered relevant for the determination of your payoffs. If a round is randomly chosen to be payoff-relevant, this means that this round will count towards your earnings. Otherwise, it will not.

You will never know whether the current round is payoff-relevant for you. However, you will be able to see whether the current round is payoff-relevant for your counterpart. You can only influence the counterpart’s earnings when it is their payoff-relevant round.

Which rounds are payoff-relevant for Player A and Player B is determined independently. This means that knowing whether the current round is payoff-relevant for your counterpart does not imply that it is more or less likely that the current round is payoff-relevant for you.

For each payoff-relevant round, the payoffs are as follows:


Player A’s payoff:

Player A can earn a bonus of {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos depending on Player B’s guess in this round. In particular, the probability of Player A earning the {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos bonus is increasing in Player B’s guess. The higher Player B’s guess is, the more likely it is that Player A will win {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos.

Simply put, Player A will earn more if Player B guesses a higher number.

If you want to see a detailed description of how your winning chance increases in Player B’s guess, please click the following button:

Player A’s payoff detailed description

Player B’s guess is a number between 1 and 6. This number is used to determine Player A’s payoff according to the following formula:

Probability of Player A receiving {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos (in percent)=

Player B's guess - 1 5 × 100

The formula takes the difference between Player B’s guess and 6. The resulting number is divided by 5 and then multiplied by 100.

Suppose that Player B’s guess is equal to 6. In this case, the difference is 5, and Player A’s probability of winning the bonus is 100%. In contrast, when Player B’s guess is 1, the difference is equal to 0. This implies that Player A’s probability of winning the bonus is 0%.

The principle underlying the formula is simple: The closer Player B’s guess is to the highest possible guess, the higher the chance that Player A receives a payoff of {{Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT}} pesos.

Player B’s payoff:

Player B can also earn a bonus of {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos depending on the accuracy of their guess. In particular, the probability of Player B earning the {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos bonus increases with the accuracy of Player B’s guess. The more accurate the guess is, the more likely it is that Player B will win {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos.

Simply put, Player B earns more when they guess a number closer to the secret number.

If you want to see a detailed description of how Player B’s winning chance increases with the accuracy of their guess, please click the following button:

Player B’s payoff detailed description

The Player B’s guess is a number between 1 and 6. This number is used to determine Player Bs’s payoff according to the following formula:

Probability of Player B receiving {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos (in percent)=

100 - 100 25 × ( Secret number - Player B's guess ) 2

The formula takes the square of the difference between Player B’s guess and the secret number. The resulting number is multiplied by a constant and then subtracted from 100.

Suppose that Player B’s guess is exactly equal to the secret number. In this case, the difference is zero, and Player B’s chance of winning the bonus is 100%. As Player B’s guess moves away from the true value of the secret number, the difference becomes larger, which decreases the probability of winning the bonus.

The principle underlying the formula is simple: The closer Player B’s guess is to the secret number, the higher the chance that Player B receives a payoff of {{ Constants.BONUS_AMOUNT }} pesos.
