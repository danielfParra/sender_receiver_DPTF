Instructions

Today, you will be randomly paired with another participant to play a game. The computer has randomly assigned you the role of Player A or Player B, which you will keep for the {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds of the experiment. We will tell you your role on the next screen. In each round, if you are a Player A, you will be randomly matched with a Player B, and vice versa. Your partner will change randomly from one round to the next. Neither of you will know the identity of the other, and all interactions will be anonymized by the computers.


General description of the game


Each round of the game has four steps.

  1. The computer generates a secret number, which is 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6.

  2. Player A sends a message to Player B saying "The secret number is X." Player A can choose any number 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 for X. For example, if Player A chooses “2”, Player B will receive the message "The secret number is 2." While choosing which message to send, Player A does not know the secret number.

  3. Player B observes the message and guesses the secret number. Player B can guess any number between 1 and 6, including two-digit numbers. For example, a Player B who thinks that 1 and 2 are equally likely can guess 1.50, and a Player B who thinks that the secret number is either 4 or 5 but most likely 5 can make a guess between 4 and 5 but closer to 5, such as 4.8.

  4. Both Player A and Player B will receive feedback. This feedback will include the secret number, the original message, and the recipient's guess.

How does the computer generate the secret number?
You can think of the computer as a device that randomly selects one of six balls from an urn that are numbered 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.


As the urn contains one ball of each number, the probability of generating any given number between 1 and 6 is equal. Consequently, in the absence of any information, you can reasonably assume that the secret number will, on average, be equal to 3.5.

These draws are completely independent across rounds. This means that you should not strategize about the value of the secret number based on the value of secret number in a previous round.
